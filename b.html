What types of DNS records can I configure?

The types of DNS records that you can configure on your domain are: A: points to an IP. The destination server is responsible for processing the incoming request. You must take into account whether the destination IP is fixed or dynamic. Example: 192.168.1.104 AAAA: it is a type of record similar to the previous one, since it also points to an IP. They differ in that they point to an IPv6 IP address. Example: 2001:0dd8:85a3:08d3:1319:8a2e:0370:7334 CNAME: points to another domain or another subdomain. Example: blog.example.com
